⚙️ Methodology

How the retirement planner calculates your projections year by year.

⚠️ This tool provides estimates for planning purposes only. It is not tax advice. Cross-border US-AU taxation is complex — always consult a tax professional qualified in both jurisdictions before making financial decisions.

Projection Model Overview

The engine runs a deterministic, year-by-year simulation from the current year to the latest life expectancy year across all household members. Each year it:

  • Grows all accounts by their configured growth rate
  • Adds contributions (while working)
  • Inflates expenses using the applicable country rate
  • Calculates income from all sources
  • Draws from accounts to cover any income gap
  • Estimates taxes under applicable jurisdictions
  • Records net worth and cash flow

Account Growth

balance_next = balance × (1 + growthRate)

Growth is applied at the start of each year before contributions or withdrawals. This is a simplified nominal return — it does not model sequence-of-returns risk, volatility, or inflation-adjusted returns separately.

Expense Inflation

expenses_year = baseExpenses × retirementRatio × (1 + inflationRate)^yearsElapsed

While you live in the US, US CPI applies. After the move year, Australian CPI applies to living costs. The base expenses are your current annual spending in USD.

Income Sources

Employment Income

Grows with US inflation while working. When one spouse retires, income is halved proportionally. Stops entirely when both are retired.

Social Security

Starts at each person's configured claiming age. Benefits grow at 2.3% annually (historical COLA average). Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable depending on combined income.

Account Withdrawals

When income + Social Security falls short of expenses, the engine draws from retirement accounts in order:

  1. 401(k) / Traditional IRA (oldest, typically)
  2. Roth IRA (last, to maximize tax-free growth)
  3. Australian Super (after move and age 60)
  4. Brokerage accounts

Early withdrawal penalties apply if withdrawing before age 59.5 (US accounts).

Tax Modeling

US Federal Income Tax

Applied to ordinary income (employment, Social Security, 401k/IRA/Roth withdrawals) using the Married Filing Jointly brackets for the selected tax base year, inflated at 2.5% annually for future years. Includes a simplified FICA component on employment income. The standard deduction is also inflated annually.

Brokerage capital gains are separated from ordinary income and taxed at preferential long-term rates rather than at ordinary bracket rates (see US Capital Gains Tax below).

US Capital Gains Tax

When brokerage accounts are drawn down in retirement, the gain portion of each withdrawal (gain fraction × withdrawal amount) is taxed at long-term capital gains rates, separately from ordinary income:

  • Long-term gains (assumed held 12+ months): 0%, 15%, or 20% based on total income level
  • Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT): 3.8% on investment income above $250K MFJ threshold
  • Short-term gains (<365 days): taxed as ordinary income (not currently triggered — all brokerage withdrawals are treated as long-term)
US total tax = calcIncomeTax(ordinaryIncome) + calcCapitalGainsTax(brokerageGain, ordinaryIncome)

Double Taxation Relief (US-Australia Treaty)

Because the US taxes worldwide income of its citizens and Australia taxes worldwide income of residents, both countries have a claim on the same income after the move. The US-Australia Tax Treaty (1982, updated 2001) provides relief in two forms, both modeled here:

Income Tax — Australian FITO

Australian income tax is reduced by the portion of US income tax already paid on the same income (Foreign Income Tax Offset). Social Security is excluded from the Australian base as it is taxable only by the US under the Totalization Agreement.

auTaxableIncome = employment + accountWithdrawals (excludes Social Security) usTaxOnAUIncome = usTax × (auTaxableIncome / totalIncome) auNetIncomeTax = max(0, auIncomeTax − usTaxOnAUIncome)

The net effect: each dollar of income is taxed at the higher of the US or Australian rate, not both in full.

Capital Gains — CGT Credit

When brokerage assets are sold post-move, both the US and Australia may tax the gain. The model applies a treaty credit: the Australian CGT liability on post-move gains is reduced by the US capital gains tax already assessed on those same gains.

auNetCGT = max(0, auCGT − usCGT)

Because Australia's CGT rates (marginal rate on 50%-discounted gain) are often close to or above US long-term rates, this credit is typically partial — you still pay some Australian CGT on gains that exceed the US tax. The result is that on any given gain, you pay the higher of the two countries' effective rates, not both in full.

Australian Income Tax (post-move)

Australian marginal rates for the selected tax base year are applied per person (Australia taxes individuals, not couples jointly). Rates are inflated at 3.0% annually. The Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) and Medicare Levy (2%) are included.

Australian Capital Gains Tax

  • Only gains accrued after establishing Australian tax residency are subject to Australian CGT — the cost basis is reset to the market value on the move date
  • Assets held >12 months: 50% CGT discount for residents (taxable gain = 50% of nominal gain)
  • Discounted gain is stacked on top of ordinary income and taxed at the marginal rate
  • Non-residents do not get the 50% discount

Australian Superannuation Tax

  • Contributions tax: 15% within the fund
  • Earnings tax in accumulation phase: 15%
  • Withdrawals after age 60: tax-free (if meeting condition of release)
  • Withdrawals before age 60: taxed at marginal rate less a 15% offset

🚨 US citizens and Super: The IRS treats Australian Super as a foreign grantor trust, meaning US income tax may apply to Super earnings annually. This is a major planning consideration. The model currently does not fully reflect this complexity — consult a specialist.

Net Worth Calculation

netWorth = Σ(accountBalances) + Σ(propertyEquity) + Σ(brokerageBalances)

Property equity = current value minus outstanding mortgage. All values are in USD; use the AUD toggle to convert at the configured FX rate.

The Australia Move Event

When the move year is reached:

  • Australian CPI replaces US CPI for expense inflation
  • Australian tax module becomes co-active alongside US filing
  • Australian Super becomes accessible (if age 60+ is met)
  • Capital gains on US assets held pre-move use a "deemed disposal" concept (simplified)

What This Model Does NOT Include

  • Sequence-of-returns risk / Monte Carlo simulation
  • Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
  • State income taxes
  • Totalization Agreement nuances (US-AU Social Security treaty)
  • Transfer Balance Cap limits on Australian Super pension phase
  • Foreign Asset Reporting (FBAR, FATCA)
  • Estate taxes / inheritance considerations
  • Medicare / private health insurance costs

These are areas where professional advice is particularly important.
